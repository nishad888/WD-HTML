Session 5 — Semantic HTML5 Tags

Why Semantic Tags Matter

Semantic tags describe the meaning of content, not just its appearance. This helps search engines understand page structure (better SEO) and lets screen readers navigate the page logically for visually impaired users (better accessibility) — a generic <div> conveys none of that.

<main>
<article> Blog Post Content
<aside> Related Links
<footer> © 2026 Site

Semantic Tags Reference

  • <header> — introductory content or site title, usually at the top
  • <nav> — navigation links
  • <main> — the primary content of the page (one per page)
  • <article> — a self-contained piece of content (blog post, news story)
  • <section> — a thematic grouping of content
  • <aside> — content tangentially related to the main content (sidebar)
  • <footer> — footer content, usually at the bottom
